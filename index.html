THE MELT

Release Date: November 2018

Role in Project: Director, Animator, Script Writer, Voice Actor

Link to view: Google Drive

"The Melt" was 3 minute short animated film that I produced for my ethics class while attending CEGEP de Sept-Îles.

The core theme of the film revolved around the disasterous concequences of climate change presented throught a humourous scope, featuring a wealthy polar bear lose his property over time due to the melting of the polar ice caps.

Software used to create the film were Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Animate, and Adobe Premiere.

WASTE INVADERS

Release Date: May 2022

Role in Project: 2D Artist, Concept Artist

Link to view: N/A

"Waste invaders" was an educational video game I produced in collaboration with a student from the Student Sustainability Ambassadors Program from Concordia University, along with other team members, including one of my friends.

The video game was a clone of the arcade game "Space Invaders" with the main difference being there being 8 projectiles the player to shoot, each of which could only take down a certain type of enemy, that in of itself corresponded to 8 different types of recycling categories.

The goal of the project was to teach players the differences between different categories of recyclable materials. After the initial build was finished, it was then uploaded into an arcade cabinet, built by the team and hand-painted by another artist.

I was the project's 2D artist and used Microsoft Paint and Adobe Photoshop to create the game's art assets and concept art, with Unity being the engine used to build the game.

MILDEW

Release Date: September 2022

Role in Project: 2D Artist, Level Designer, Programmer

Link to view: Itch.io

"Mildew" is an upcoming video game project I have been working on along with one of my friends since the fall of 2022.

The project was originally created during the game jam GBJam 10, with my friend and I expanding upon the project much later on, and currently, to this day.

The video game itself is a puzzle platformer featuring a sentient puff of mildew wandering around a park clearing it of its mold. The player is able to jump into differnt plant pots and take control of them, using the plant's special abilities to solve puzzles.

I was the artist and level designer for this project. Microsoft Paint and Adobe Photoshop were used to create the game's art assets and concept art, and the game ran on the Unity engine.

QUANTUM CAT-ASTROPHE

Release Date: October 2022

Role in Project: 2D Artist, Level Designer

Link to view: Itch.io

"Quantum Cat-astrophe" is a puzzle platformer originally made for CGDjam back in October of 2022, and was later expanded upon for the following month.

The game centers around a cat exploring a seemingly abandoned underground laboratory searching for one of their friends- who is a dog. The player would need to kill themselves using traps placed throughout the level in order to become a ghost and solve certain puzzles the cat cannot while still alive.

I was the game's artist and also touched up some of the level desgin as well. I used Microsoft Paint and Adobe Photohop in order to create the game's art assets.

RUN FOR THE SUN

Release Date: July 2023

Role in Project: 2D Artist, Level Designer, Programmer

Link to view: Itch.io

"Run for the Sun" is a video game that was originally concieved during Global Game Jam 2023. Unfortunately, due to technical issues during development, the game never got finished in time, and the orignal build was unable to be submitted.

However! Despite that hiccup, myself and two other programmers on our team decided to continue on with developing the game, eventually releasing it in July 2023.

As for the game itself, "Run for the Sun" is about "Sunny" a sunflower seed who mysteriously gains a conciousness. From there, the player, using their mouse controls Sunny by shooting their roots out around the level, to eventually climb all the way to the top, as fast as possible.

My role in this project was originally solely as an aritst, however as development progressed I began to take on the role of level designer, and did a small bit of programming as well. This project was made in Unity, and I made the game's art with Microsoft Paint and Adobe Photohop.

DIVINE AFFAIRE

Release Date: November 2023

Role in Project: 2D Artist

Link to view: Itch.io

"Divine Affaire", originally "Chroniquer", is a research project based around a massive simulation, featuring simulated people interacting with each other and living about their lives. The project has a heavy focus on emergent narrtives, and getting users to interact with those narratives.

The simulation runs in the Unity Engine, and while the simulation itself is complete for the time being, the focus of the research project now is to create a game over-top of it, to act as a front-end to get users to interact and discover the emergent narratives that the simulation creates.

I was brought onto the project in the summer of 2023, however, the project has been going on for a few years before that.

On this project, I've made a number of art assets, however only a few are present in the currently public build, with 99% of them being for a feature planned to be implemented in the coming months.

FROGDROMEDA

Release Date: April 2024

Role in Project: Programmer, 2D Artist

Link to view: N/A

"FROGDROMEDA" is a arcade-style space shooter video game orignally created for one of my university classes.

In this game, players take control of a frog (in a frog-shaped spaceship) in space, who's tasked with destroying evil space bugs. Unlike other space shooters where players use bullets to deal with enemies, in FROGDROMEDA, players use their tongue to grab enemies, where they can shoot them back out afterward. The player also has access to an "inventory" where they can hold multiple enemies in their mouth at a time.

The game was exhibited at the DCART end of year exhibition held at Concordia University. The game was played using a joystick controller with a mouse, however the current build can be played using keyboard controls, as well.

I still have plans to continue working on this game in the future, but there's no real timeframe I can give at this moment where I can say the "finished" version of the game will be released.

FROGDROMEDA was developed using the Unity game engine, and for the art assets I used Microsoft Paint and Adobe Photoshop, per usual.

REVERIE RECIPIES

Release Date: May 2024

Role in Project: 2D Artist, 3D Artist

Link to view: N/A

"Reverie Recipies" is a co-op video game created during the Ubisoft GameLab 2024 competition.

The game's plot revolves around a chef, on his deathbed, passing onto his son his restaurant. The catch? His son doesn't know how to cook. From there, the son returns home and goes to bed, only to see his father in his dream, and from there, learns how to cook.

The game can be best described as a 3D hack 'n slash, where players need to kill and collect evil sentient ingredients to cook up a food dish, which manifests as a boss which players also have to defeat as well. Each player is rewarded with a new weapon upon completing each round. So far, there are only two rounds, but there have been talks to continue developing the game further and adding additional levels. However, at the time of writing this, I haven't recieved any updates from my peers regarding continuing the project.

The game was made using the Unity game engine, and the art assets that I created (those being some of the enemies and the latter portion of the opening cutscene) were created using Blender and Blockbench for the 3D models, and Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Premiere for the cutscene art.

FLEAS!

Release Date: May 2024

Role in Project: Programmer, 2D Artist

Link to view: Github.io

"FLEAS!" is a web-game developed using p5.js.

In it, players play as a flea bouncing on a set of blocks which are both endlessly shifting and rotating. The player's goal is to survive as long as possible.

FLEAS! was orignially created for a University course, and I continued developing the game after the course had wrapped up. I plan to continue developing the game further, as there are still multiple feature I have yet to add.

The game was created using the Phaser 3, and Matter.js p5.js libraries, and for the art assets, I used Adobe Photoshop.
